xose segovia martin

I am interested in the relationship between cognition and society. In particular, I investigate the effects of cognitive biases and network topologies on the evolution of the convergence of cultural conventions. My research uses both computer modelling and experiments in the lab to understand how the interaction between individual biases and social structures, rather than just the aggregate preferences of individual actors, leads to a concrete distribution of cultural variants. I've been involved in a postgraduate research program at the Centre for Language Evolution (University of Edinburgh) and now I spend most of my time working in an interdisciplinary PhD program in Cognitive Science organised by the University of Barcelona and the Unversitat Autònoma de Barcelona. I am also a member of the "Development, Acquisition, and Mechanisms of Linguistic Variation research unit" at UAB. I also hold a teaching position in general science at Fundación Estudio since 2011.
